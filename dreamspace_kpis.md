# DreamSpace — KPIs & Project Context Reference

## About DreamSpace

DreamSpace is a digital literacy and technology education initiative for youth aged 8–18, operating in the Netherlands. It positions itself as an independent, mission-driven programme focused on equipping the next generation with STEM skills, computational thinking, and 21st-century competencies.

> **Narrative note:** DreamSpace operates with deliberate brand independence. In the current European climate of growing scrutiny toward big tech, the programme's value lies partly in being perceived as a community-first education initiative — not a corporate marketing vehicle. Communication should lead with **youth impact and social mission**, not corporate association.

## Business KPIs

### 1. Reach & Volume
*The headline numbers that justify programme funding and continuation.*

- **Students reached** per academic year (primary metric for stakeholder reporting)
- **Workshops / sessions delivered** (volume indicator)
- **Schools engaged** (institutional penetration)
- **Geographic spread** — urban centres vs. underserved regions (e.g., Rotterdam Zuid, peripheral municipalities)

### 2. Teacher Enablement
*Teachers are the scaling mechanism. One trained teacher multiplies impact across years and cohorts.*

- Number of **teachers trained** or certified through the programme
- **Independent adoption rate** — teachers delivering content in their own classrooms without facilitation
- Teacher **confidence scores** in digital/STEM delivery (pre/post)
- Number of **teacher packages downloaded or activated**

### 3. Student Learning Outcomes
*Demonstrates actual educational impact beyond attendance.*

- Pre/post **STEM interest and confidence** scores
- **Digital literacy skill improvement** (assessed or self-reported)
- **Engagement and completion rates** during sessions
- Curriculum alignment with the **Dutch SLO framework**

### 4. Equity & Inclusion
*A core programme pillar — and a key differentiator in funding narratives.*

- % of participants from **lower socioeconomic backgrounds**
- % **female participation** in technology-focused activities
- Reach in **underserved or disadvantaged communities**
- Representation of students with **non-Western migration backgrounds**

### 5. Programme Efficiency
*Operational KPIs for internal performance and cost justification.*

- **Cost per student reached**
- **Sessions per facilitator**
- **Repeat engagement rate** — schools returning for multiple sessions
- Volunteer/partner hours contributed (leverage ratio)

## Relevance to Intern Tasks & Project Work

When delivering tasks as a DreamSpace intern or advancing the capstone project, keep the following in mind:

### Reporting & Impact Documentation
- Always **quantify student and teacher reach** when summarising programme activities
- Frame outputs in terms of **social impact first**, operational metrics second
- Aggregated class performance data and engagement metrics are expected deliverables for stakeholder and funding reports — build these into any platform or tool you design

### Product / Intervention Design
- Any digital tool (e.g., the adaptive quiz platform) should be designed to **surface KPI-relevant data automatically** — session completion, pre/post scores, class-level performance trends
- Teacher usability is not optional — it is a **primary KPI driver** (teacher adoption = programme scale)
- Align question content and outcomes to the **SLO curriculum framework** to support educational legitimacy claims

### Communication & Tone
- Avoid leading with tech company association in external-facing content
- Use language like: *"an initiative empowering youth through digital education"* rather than corporate-branded framing
- Equity and inclusion narratives (girls in tech, underserved communities) should be **foregrounded** in any public-facing or press-ready output
- The programme's credibility rests on **research-backed, community-embedded** positioning — reinforce this in any documentation or presentation

### Stakeholder Alignment
- **Maud Pols** (programme lead) — primary internal stakeholder for programme delivery and reporting
- **Microsoft NL leadership** — require aggregated impact data that maps to CSR and funding commitments
- **Schools and teachers** — end users; their experience and adoption rates are a direct proxy for programme success
